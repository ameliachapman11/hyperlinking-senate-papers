# Reflection
The following is a reflection of my time as an intern in the LFCS, completed on the last day of the internship.

## What I Can Take Away from this Experience
I learned a lot about web development, a field that I am interested in pursuing in the future. In terms of building a website, I became aware of various tools often used in industry that can expedite the development process, such as frontend and backend frameworks, and understanding which scenarios they are most appropriate for. I became familiar with different popular CMSs and had the opportunity to begin building a WordPress website.

In terms of coding, I gained experience working with new Python libraries and understanding their capabilities. I learned the importance of thoroughly checking output to recognize small logical errors that may not be initially apparent.

I gained experience writing formalized documentation to communicate my findings. I would say this was one of the most important takeaways because it made me realize the importance of only including the necessary details. For example, in the Build Approach document, I could have included an evaluation of each individual DAM I looked at when researching DAMs, but I realized it was more important to just describe *what* a DAM is and *why* it was not relevant to the project. For someone else reading the documentation, they only really need to know that it is not appropriate rather than getting a complete crash course of what implementing a DAM would look like.

Finally, at the end of internship party/talk, I gained experience presenting my work in a concise manner that is accessible for those who have limited background knowledge in the area, as well as confidently answering questions regarding the presentation. I had to spend quite a bit of time preparing the presentation as summarizing 8 weeks of work into 5 minutes is not an easy task&mdash;I had to continually ask myself "Do they need to know this specific detail?" or "What is the main takeaway rather than the exact process?"

## What I Would Do Differently (**to finish)
### "Is this productive?"
The main thing I would change about my work process throughout the internship is to ask myself more frequently "Is what I'm doing right now productive?" Upon reflection, there were certain aspects of what I completed that could have been done more quickly while retaining the same takeaway. 

For example, when I was evaluating different CMSs, I ended up watching an entire YouTube video of someone describing how to add custom styling/changing fonts and colors in a SharePoint site. For my purposes, I only needed to know what SharePoint was *capable of* rather than exactly how to implement it. Another example is that when I was looking into different search engines, I began watching a video of someone explaining how to install Solr on a Drupal site. While my purpose was to understand how easy integration of Solr was in Drupal, it was not something I needed to know exactly how to do at that point in time. 

I believe a better way to approach work is to gather options and worry about the implementation later. While it is good to fully understand your options before you commit to a decision, you can overprepare. It is better to try things out and see if you can make it work rather than worrying about making the perfect decision, especially in an environment where our goal is just prototyping.

### Blocking Time/Specific Goals



* Be more precise in my goals/deadlines (say "by the end of today I want to have finished dealing with links in the papers" rather than just generally saying "I'm going to continue working on the PDF to XML script"
* Ask myself "is what I'm doing productive?" I ended up taking multiple weeks of the internship to develop a script which doesn't even completely render things 100% of the time... probably should have instead communicated these struggles to my supervisor and asked if it was okay for me to start development instead. 
* Read more documentation rather than relying on ELM for suggestions (ex. deciding which Python library to use for PDF content extraction)
* Be more realistic with limitations (for some reason I was originally quite confident that I would be able to produce a working Drupal site despite the fact that I had never used it before...). Communicate these limitations frequently to those who might be able to help you.
* Time management: use blocks to structure my day (ex. work on a certain thing for an hour... if it's not done by then, move on to something else and come back later). Makes sure I can get a wide variety of things done rather than focusing too heavily on one thing. Sometimes it's better to take a break and come back to things.
